Our story

What an unlikely union, huh? Pranava grew up in a large city of Hyderabad in India, and Katya - in a small town of Sosnovy Bor in Russia. Pranava is a vegetarian; Katya used to consider her meal incomplete without meat. Pranava loves origami and puzzles; Katya - theater and arts. What do we have in common? Physics! (No, for realâ€¦)

We met in 2014 in Chicago while studying physics at IIT (not the Indian one), and have been together ever since. In these past few years, we got 3 degrees (4th in progress) and lived in 5 cities (and visited a dozen more). Katya learned to eat spicy vegetarian food, tolerate cilantro, and acquired limited knowledge of pop culture. Pranava got limited proficiency in Russian swears, but still falls asleep at just about any musical he attends.

Most importantly, together we made lots of wonderful memories and friends. We are happy to (finally) get married and hope you can join us in celebrating this very special occasion!

What to expect at the event

Wedding rituals

This will an abridged version of traditional telugu wedding. However, it will still take place over two days with the main event to be held on September 1. More information about the important rituals could be found here. The time for wedding is very strictly defined for the bride and the groom but is flexible for the guests. We would love for you to be present for the whole event, however, we request that you atleast arrive by 10:30 AM and stay for the lunch.

Etiquette and Dress code

We request you to respect the temple rules. You will be required to take off shoes and place them in a designated area before entering the wedding hall, please plan accordingly. There is no dress code but we request that you respect the rules of temple and dress respectfully. We however encourage you to wear colorful clothes and those of you who are inclined to wear ethnic indian wear which include bright and vibrant colors.

The atmoshere will be casual otherwise with and there will not be assigned seating and guests are welcome to walk in and out as desired. Indian weddings are quite spirited and you are encouraged to pariticate in the chaotic energy by engaging in conversations in addition to engaging with the rituals.
We are more than happy to help with any questions you may have about the etiquette and dressing.

Gifting

There is no wedding registry, and we do not expect any gifts. We are really looking foward to have the wedding in your company. If you really insist on gifting, we prefer money over material gifts.

What to expect at the event

Please note that this is a very casual event (no assigned seating, no bridesmaids), so please don't over-dress.

There is no wedding registry, and we do not expect any gifts. We are really looking to commemorate our wedding by having a nice evening in a company of friends and family. If you really insist on giving something, we encourage you to give money.

If you play a musical instrument, please consider bringing it with you for us to enjoy your skills.

Bapu bommalu


The late Sri. Sattiraju Lakshminarayana, more commonly referred to as Bapu, was an acclaimed telugu director, cartoonist, and illustrator among other things. 'Bapu bomma', literally translates to 'Bapu's Illustration', and is synonymous to quintessential telugu beauty, most typically used in the cotext of describing a woman. Checkout more artwork by Bapu at the Bapu art collection website. Checkout this wikipedia page to learn more about Bapu.
